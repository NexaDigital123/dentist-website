Common Questions

Everything you
need to know

We believe an informed patient is a confident patient. Here are the questions we hear most often. Can't find your answer?

Ask Us Directly
How long do porcelain veneers last?
+
With proper care, our E.max porcelain veneers typically last 15–20 years. We use the same ceramic trusted by Hollywood's top smile designers, with a proven track record of durability and colour stability. All veneers come with a 5-year structural guarantee.
Is the consultation really free?
+
Absolutely. Your initial consultation — including a full smile assessment, 3D digital smile preview, and personalised treatment plan — is completely complimentary. We believe you should see your result before making any decision.
Will veneers or implants look natural?
+
Undetectably so. Our master ceramist hand-characterises each restoration to match your unique tooth texture, translucency, and light reflection. Even your dentist friends won't be able to tell.
Do you offer payment plans?
+
Yes. We partner with CareCredit and Sunbit to offer 0% APR financing for up to 36 months on all cosmetic treatments. Our patient concierge will walk you through all options — with no pressure.
How long does a smile makeover take?
+
Most smile makeovers are completed in 2–4 appointments over 3–6 weeks. Complex cases involving implants or orthodontics may require a longer timeline. We'll give you an exact schedule at your consultation.
Do you treat anxious or nervous patients?
+
Our entire practice philosophy is built around comfort. We offer oral sedation, nitrous oxide, warm blankets, noise-cancelling headphones, and a team specially trained in treating previously avoidant patients.
What is the difference between veneers and crowns?
+
Veneers cover only the front surface of a tooth and require minimal enamel removal — ideal for cosmetic changes. Crowns cap the entire tooth and are used when structural damage is present. Dr. Voss will always recommend the most conservative option.
Is teeth whitening safe for sensitive teeth?
+
Yes. We use a custom-adjusted protocol for sensitive patients, including desensitising gel applied before and after the ZOOM session. Over 90% of our sensitive patients report zero post-treatment discomfort.
